Group Presentation

The guidelines for our group presentation on Numerical Analysis are as follows:

Overview

  • Topic Selection: As a team, engage in a discussion to select a single topic related to Numerical Analysis. Ensure that all members participate in this decision-making process.
  • Task Allocation: Allocate research tasks related to your chosen topic among the group members. All research work should be distributed fairly, with clear responsibilities. The research work must be completed by July 17th, 2026 in preparation for presentation.
  • Teamwork: The success of this project hinges on the collective effort of all group members, so effective communication and mutual support are essential throughout the process.

Requirements

  • Duration: The total duration of the presentation should not exceed 20 minutes. The presentation should be concise and engaging to effectively communicate your findings.
  • Presentation Format: The presentation can be delivered by a single representative or multiple members of the group. However, it is vital to clearly indicate each member’s contribution to the presentation.
  • Utilization of Generative AI: We recommend strategically utilizing generative AI in research and presentation preparation. Include in your presentation content the innovations that led to improved outcomes through AI utilization, such as prompt design, model selection, and automation scripts. Additionally, maintain dialogue logs and environmental information, and appropriately implement copyright and hallucination countermeasures (verification of primary sources).
  • Reflection Section: Include a “Reflection” section at the end of your presentation using 1–2 slides. In this section, share your thoughts on unique experiences in group work, challenging aspects, lessons learned, and growth as a team.

Roles and Workflow

Always keep your own role within the group clear as you work. However, because this project runs over a short period, we recommend not fixing rigid assignments: shift roles by phase and work in parallel as much as possible. The following is only one example.

  • Phase 1 (Research and Design): As a team, divide the survey of prior work and standard methods/experimental settings, and agree on the evaluation metrics, experimental conditions, comparison targets, and the number of figures to present. Use of generative AI is encouraged (beware of misinformation and hallucinations, and always verify primary sources).
  • Phase 2 (Parallel Work): Proceed in parallel with organizing the theory, implementing code and running experiments, and analyzing and visualizing results. Share results frequently, even at intermediate stages, so that no one has to wait for the implementation to be finished.
  • Phase 3 (Integration and Presentation Preparation): Once the experiments settle down, some members move on to the slides and reproducibility write-ups, while those responsible for analysis move on to integrating the conclusions. The AI usage log and the Reflection are organized and shared by everyone, and the presentation is delivered by all members together.
